===== Mobile & Field Recording Systems =====

==== Primary Field Recorder: Sound Devices MixPre-10 II ====

*Why I Chose This Unit:*

The MixPre-10 II serves as the heart of my mobile recording operations. After extensive testing of various portable recorders, the MixPre-10 II emerged as the optimal balance of channel count, audio quality, and portability.

*Key Features I Rely On:*

  * *32-bit Float Recording*: This revolutionary feature has eliminated my anxiety about gain staging in unpredictable field conditions. Whether capturing a whisper or an explosion, the signal is recoverable in post-production.

  * *Kashmir Preamps*: With -130dBV equivalent input noise, these preamps handle everything from ribbon microphones to hot condenser mics without compromise. The ultra-low noise floor is essential for quiet nature recordings.

  * *8 Microphone Inputs*: Sufficient for stereo ambisonic arrays, multi-perspective wildlife recording, or small ensemble music capture.

  * *USB Audio Interface*: When stationary, the MixPre-10 II integrates directly with my DAW as a 12-in/4-out interface, eliminating the need for separate studio hardware on location.

  * *Timecode*: Essential for sync with video on documentary projects.

*Power Configuration:*
  * Primary: Sony NP-F970 L-mount batteries (approximately 6-8 hours)
  * Backup: 8x lithium AA batteries
  * Extended: USB-C power bank for marathon sessions

*Typical Applications:*
  * Documentary film sound
  * Nature and wildlife recording
  * Live music capture (small ensembles)
  * Podcast recording on location

=== Audient ASP880 ===

*Role in the Rig:*

The ASP880 provides eight additional microphone preamps when the MixPre-10 II's eight inputs aren't sufficient, but before deploying the full Orion 32+ system. It bridges the gap perfectly.

*Key Specifications:*
  * 8 Class-A discrete microphone preamps
  * Variable input impedance (200Ω to 14.4kΩ)
  * Individual phantom power per channel
  * Per-channel insert points for outboard processing
  * ADAT optical output (to Fireface UFX+)

*Why Variable Impedance Matters:*

Different microphones respond optimally to different input impedances:
  * *Low impedance (200-600Ω)*: Brightens ribbon microphones
  * *Medium impedance (1.2-2.4kΩ)*: Neutral for most dynamics
  * *High impedance (10kΩ+)*: Opens up some condensers

I frequently adjust impedance when tracking to fine-tune microphone response without EQ.

*Insert Usage:*
The hardware inserts allow me to patch in analog compressors or EQs during tracking—essential for my approach to capturing performances with appropriate dynamics control.

=== Zoom F3 ===

*Role in My Kit:*

The F3 is my "always with me" recorder. Its compact size, 32-bit float recording, and exceptional preamp quality make it the perfect grab-and-go solution.

*Key Specifications:*
  * 2 XLR inputs with professional preamps
  * 32-bit float recording (dual A/D converter design)
  * Compact, lightweight design
  * USB-C power option

*Why 32-bit Float Changes Everything:*

Before 32-bit float, field recording required constant attention to levels. Unexpected loud sounds could clip, ruining irreplaceable recordings. The F3's dual A/D system captures both high and low gain simultaneously, allowing level adjustment in post-production.

*Real-World Example:*
Recording wildlife, I set the F3 and walk away. Whether a distant owl or a nearby branch snap, the recording is usable. This freedom is revolutionary.

*Typical Applications:*
  * Wildlife recording (set-and-forget)
  * Backup recorder with safety levels
  * Travel recording
  * Quick capture when opportunity arises

===== Microphone Collection =====

==== Dynamic Microphones ====

=== Shure SM57 ===

*Quantity:* 4

*Role in My Collection:*

The SM57 is arguably the most versatile microphone ever made. After 60+ years, it remains the industry standard for snare drum, guitar amplifiers, and countless other sources.

*Technical Characteristics:*
  * Type: Dynamic cardioid
  * Frequency Response: 40Hz - 15kHz
  * Impedance: 150Ω (310Ω actual)
  * Sensitivity: -54.5dBV/Pa (1.88mV)

*My SM57 Applications:*
  * *Guitar Amplifiers*: Positioned slightly off-axis, 1-2 inches from the grille. The SM57 handles high SPL without distortion and captures the midrange punch that cuts through mixes.
  * *Snare Drum*: Angled at the edge of the drum, it captures attack and body without excessive ring.
  * *Brass Instruments*: The controlled high-frequency response tames harsh harmonics.
  * *Backup Vocal*: When rejection is more important than ultimate fidelity.

*Why Multiple SM57s:*
Having four allows drum kit multi-miking (snare, toms) or stereo guitar amp techniques without compromising other applications.

---

=== Shure SM57A (Alpha Modification) ===

*Quantity:* 2

*What Is the Alpha Modification:*

The "Alpha" modification involves replacing the SM57's standard transformer with a higher-quality Jensen transformer and opening up the frequency response. The result retains the SM57's character while extending both low and high frequencies.

*Sonic Differences from Stock SM57:*
  * Extended low-frequency response (tighter, deeper bass)
  * Extended high-frequency response (more air and detail)
  * Slightly higher output
  * More "open" sound overall

*My Alpha SM57 Applications:*
  * Premium guitar amp recording when more detail is desired
  * Snare drum when I want additional top-end shimmer
  * Any application where SM57 character is wanted but with enhanced fidelity

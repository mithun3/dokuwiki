## Honourable Mentions: Solid-State Classics

While these Boss pedals are legendary for **guitar** applications, their circuit design makes them less suitable for full-spectrum work.

### Boss SD-1 Super Overdrive

**The Workhorse (Since 1981)**

One of the longest-running pedals in Boss's catalog, the SD-1 has been on countless pedalboards for over 40 years.

**Famous Users:**
- Stevie Ray Vaughan
- Zakk Wylde (as a boost)
- John Frusciante (Red Hot Chili Peppers)
- Robert Smith (The Cure)
- J Mascis (Dinosaur Jr.)

**Why It's Great for Guitar:**
- Asymmetric clipping produces tube-like harmonics
- Mid-hump helps guitar cut through a band mix
- Touch-sensitive dynamics

**Why It's NOT for Bass/Synth:**
- 720Hz high-pass filter **before** the clipping stage
- Bass below 720Hz receives **progressively less distortion**
- Synth sub-bass becomes thin and lifeless

### Boss OD-2 Turbo Overdrive

**The 80s Icon (1985-1995)**

The OD-2 added a "Turbo" switch to the classic OD-1 circuit, offering higher gain options.

**Famous Users:**
- The Edge (U2)
- Johnny Marr (The Smiths)
- Prince

**Same Limitations Apply:**
- Tube Screamer-based circuit
- Filters bass frequencies before clipping
- Designed specifically for guitar's frequency range
